30-Day Study Plan

A clear day-by-day schedule — no guessing what to study next

📅 1 hour per day is enough — consistency beats intensity
The Rules Read first

⏱ 1 hour per day minimum

You don't need 8-hour grind sessions. 1 focused hour every day beats 8 hours once a week. Consistency is everything in learning to code.

🛠 Build every mini project

Reading is not learning. Every topic in the guides has a mini project — build it. Your hands need to know the code, not just your eyes.

📖 Read guide → Tips → Build

The pattern for every topic: read the guide section, read the tip for that topic, then build the mini project. Don't skip the tips — they prevent the mistakes.

🐛 Embrace the bugs

When something breaks, don't immediately Google it. Spend 10 minutes debugging yourself first. This is how real skill is built — not by avoiding errors, but by fixing them.

📊 Track your progress

Use the Progress Tracker to check off topics as you complete them. Seeing the bar fill up keeps you motivated and shows you exactly where you are.

🔁 Review beats re-reading

Don't re-read chapters you've done. Instead, build something from memory and use the cheatsheet when you get stuck. Active recall is 10x more effective than passive re-reading.
